## ðŸ§ª Behavioral Economics at Play

### 1. **Sunk Cost Fallacy**
**Definition:** Having invested time/money, we're reluctant to abandon it

**How we use it:**
- Lifetime usage counter (shows total investment)
- Model training history (can't lose this progress)
- Tier status (took time to earn this discount)

**Customer thought:** "I've already put $10k and 6 months into this. Can't switch now."

### 2. **Loss Aversion**
**Definition:** Losses hurt more than equivalent gains feel good

**How we use it:**
- "Lose your 75% discount if you switch"
- "Back to $0.01/example elsewhere"
- "Restart tier progress from zero"

**Customer thought:** "Switching = losing all my progress. Can't do it."

### 3. **The Endowment Effect**
**Definition:** We value things more once we own them

**How we use it:**
- "Your tier status"
- "Your API key" (feels like property)
- "Your discount level" (earned it, it's mine)

**Customer thought:** "This is MY discount. I earned this."

### 4. **Variable Reward Schedule**
**Definition:** Unpredictable rewards are more addictive than fixed ones

**How we use it:**
- Model improvement varies (not always linear)
- Occasional bonus offers
- Surprise milestone unlocks
- Sometimes big jumps in performance

**Customer thought:** "What if THIS batch is the breakthrough?"

### 5. **Anchoring Bias**
**Definition:** First number we see influences perception of value

**How we use it:**
- Start at $0.01/example (the anchor)
- Show savings vs this anchor always
- "Originally $3,000, you paid $900"

**Customer thought:** "I'm saving so much vs the original price!"

### 6. **Social Proof**
**Definition:** We do what others do

**How we use it:**
- "500+ companies use GOTCHA"
- Case studies with big names
- Public leaderboard (optional)
- Tier badges visible to team

**Customer thought:** "If Google uses this, it must be good."

### 7. **Commitment & Consistency**
**Definition:** We act consistently with our previous choices

**How we use it:**
- First small purchase (commitment)
- Then consistent increases (stay consistent)
- Public tier status (committed identity)

**Customer thought:** "I'm a Power User. I act like one."
